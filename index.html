Animal Pictograms

CLASS PROJECT

The project was based on simplifying representational objects and abstracting them while still maintaining their readability. We were assigned to pick an animal from each of the three categories: Mammals, Reptiles, and Rodents. I chose the narwhal, sea turtle, and flying squirrel, respectively. I took inspiration from Asian calligraphy, and wanted to represent each animal with as few lines as possible while using brush strokes to give it texture and character. It was completed digitally, but I started the thumbnails and sketches on paper and scanned in the brush strokes.

Through this project, I learned the importance of simplifying shapes by getting rid of their non-essential elements, as well as making a set of images which follow a specific style. I learned how important the concept of simplification was in graphic design, and especially in creating logos. Because pictograms are used to communicate a message or idea without the use of words, I had to consider who the viewer would be and whether the image would be read the same across different cultures.

I used the Christoph Niemann's Abstractometer as a rough barometer to establish whether what I created was too realistic or abstract.

These are some thumbnails and iterations I used. I first started out with silhouettes and filled in shapes, but that eventually turned into all lines.

These were the final versions.

27 Cafe

BRANDING
POSTER DESIGN

27 Café is a hipster poetry café in Beijing, China popular with younger Chinese. We presented the concept of branding the café after the “Dada” art movement by creating imagery using techniques such as Collage, Readymades, and Photomontages mixed in with poetry presented in a vibrant and quirky way. I was in charge of making a large, vertical poster that would be placed outside to advertise the café. In addition, I helped design the interior by decorating the inside with famous poetry quotes.

These are the versions of the posters I created. I wanted to incorporate an old, nostalgic feel to them while adding the whimsical Dada elements through hand-drawn illustrations and collage. The first version was the one which the client ended up choosing.

The inside of the cafe would be decorated with the company's branding and poetry quotes on frosted vinyl.

Webpower Asia

WEBSITE DESIGN

Webpower Asia is a global online marketing company with headquarters in both Spain and China that sent more than 12 billion emails in 2016 and is used by industry leaders like Disney, Alibaba, Huawei, and BMW, among others. They needed a new website for their Chinese platform and I helped design their landing page. The landing page has a large header with a clear call-to-action and is designed the company’s color scheme to fit their branding. It was launched in fall of 2017.

Since a lot of the company's features were done through mobile, I wanted to include the company's features on a smartphone to emphasize their mobile-centric approach in the hero banner. In the first wireframe, I wanted two CTA's so there were multiple opportunities to engage the user. The client wanted wanted the layout to be clean and professional, and this was achieved by establishing a clear vertical directionality and visual hierarchy.
